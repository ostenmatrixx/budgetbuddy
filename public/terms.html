BudgetBuddy Privacy

Production-Beta Terms

Effective July 23, 2026

These baseline terms describe the BudgetBuddy reference deployment. Operators must obtain appropriate legal review and add their identity, contact details, governing law, and any jurisdiction-specific disclosures before opening a public service.

Personal budgeting tool

BudgetBuddy helps organize information you provide. It is not a bank, accounting service, investment adviser, tax adviser, or substitute for professional financial advice. You are responsible for reviewing records and financial decisions.

Accounts and acceptable use

Provide accurate account information, protect your credentials, and notify the operator of suspected compromise. Do not attempt to access another user’s data, disrupt the service, bypass security controls, automate abusive traffic, or use the service unlawfully.

Your data

You retain responsibility for the data you enter. You authorize the service to process it only as needed to provide and secure BudgetBuddy. You can export or delete your account from Account Settings, subject to encrypted backup expiration described in the Privacy Notice.

Beta availability

Features may change, experience interruptions, or contain defects during the beta. Keep independent records of information you cannot afford to lose and periodically use the export tools. The operator may restrict abusive accounts or suspend the service for security and maintenance.

Warranty and liability baseline

To the extent permitted by applicable law, the service is provided as available without guarantees of uninterrupted operation or fitness for a particular financial purpose. An operator must replace this baseline with terms appropriate to its jurisdiction and risk profile.

Changes and contact

Material changes should be published with a new effective date. For the reference repository, use a private GitHub security advisory for security matters.
